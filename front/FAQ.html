What is the Mifflin equation?
The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is a formula used to estimate the number of calories your body needs at rest, also known as the Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). It takes into account your age, gender, weight, and height to provide a more accurate estimate compared to other formulas.
What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?
Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the amount of energy expended by the body while at rest in a neutrally temperate environment, in the post-absorptive state (meaning that the digestive system is inactive, typically 12 hours after eating). It represents the minimum amount of energy needed to maintain basic physiological functions such as breathing, circulation, cell production, and maintenance of body temperature.
In other words, BMR is the number of calories your body requires to perform essential functions while at rest. It's often expressed in calories per day. BMR is influenced by several factors, including age, gender, weight, height, and body composition. Generally, BMR decreases with age and tends to be higher in individuals with more lean muscle mass.
What is an Activity Factor?
The activity factor is a number that is used to multiply with your Basal Metabolic Rate. This helps accounts for extra calories burned throughout the day. It gives a more accurate reading of how much daily Calories one would need.
For example, if we take a 150lbs male athlete and a 150lbs average male that are both the same age, they will have the same BMR. However, since the athlete does more activities throughout the day, they burn more calories. They need to replace those lost calories, which is why their caloric needs are higher. The activity level will account for the activities for each person and will help calculate an accurate reading of caloric needs.
